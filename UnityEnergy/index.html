Since the first AC reactive field emerged in 1891 at the King Gold Mine in Telluride, Colorado, industry has delivered power — but never fully controlled the reactive field it created.

In 2001, a different path was discovered. After decades of field study and verification, complete industrial energy field harmonization is now achievable — without auxiliary power and while actively removing destructive harmonics at the load.

For the first time, reactive energy becomes measurable, manageable, and recoverable. The same usable power is delivered with lower electrical input, reduced heat, extended equipment life, and reclaimed system capacity.

Reactive energy was once an unavoidable liability. It is now a controllable asset.
