🧲

Faraday Discovers Induction

1831 · Royal Institution, London

Michael Faraday reveals electromagnetic induction — proving that motion in a magnetic field can create electrical current. This is the foundational doorway into the modern field age.

👶

James Clerk Maxwell Is Born

June 13, 1831 · Edinburgh, Scotland

Maxwell is born 77 days before Faraday's electromagnetic induction demonstration. In this timeline, these twin 1831 moments frame discovery and the future mathematics that would later describe the field.

⚙️

Jacobi Demonstrates Motorized Electric Work

1834–1838 · St. Petersburg

Moritz Hermann von Jacobi develops one of the first practical rotating electric motors and demonstrates electric propulsion on the Neva in 1838, proving field theory could produce useful mechanical output.

📐

Maxwell Unifies the Field

1861 · Electromagnetic Theory Formalized

James Clerk Maxwell provides the mathematical framework unifying electricity, magnetism, and light. The field becomes quantifiable, teachable, and engineerable.

📘

Heaviside Reads Maxwell's Treatise

1873 · Treatise Era · Early Heaviside Exposure

Maxwell's two-volume Treatise circulates broadly, and Oliver Heaviside studies it in this period. He later begins reform work in 1882, eventually translating the equations into engineer-ready vector form.

💡

Edison Files Incandescent Lamp Patent

November 4, 1879 · U.S. Patent Application Filed

Edison files the patent application for an improved high-resistance incandescent lamp, accelerating commercial momentum around practical electric lighting systems.

🕯️

James Clerk Maxwell Dies

November 5, 1879 · Cambridge, England

One day after Edison's filing date, Maxwell dies at age 48. His field framework endures and quickly becomes the intellectual backbone of the industrial AC era that follows.

🏛️

Edison Lamp Patent Is Granted

January 27, 1880 · U.S. Patent 223,898

The Edison lamp patent is granted, creating a legal and commercial platform for rapid investment in generation, distribution, and electrical infrastructure buildout.

🧩

Heaviside Begins Reforming Maxwell's Equations

1882 · Initial Reform Work Begins

After years absorbing Maxwell's Treatise, Heaviside starts active reform work in 1882, setting up the equation streamlining that matures through the mid-1880s.

🧮

Oliver Heaviside Streamlines Maxwell's Equations

1884–1885 · London, England

Oliver Heaviside recasts Maxwell's original formulation into the compact vector form engineers can directly use. This translation makes field equations practical for industrial design, transmission analysis, and controls.

👨‍🔧

William Stanley Jr. — First Practical AC Field

1886 · Great Barrington, Massachusetts

Great Barrington, Massachusetts marks a turning point: William Stanley Jr. demonstrates practical AC distribution, transforming field theory into live industrial reality. This milestone anchors the origin story for modern distributed electrical infrastructure.

📡

Heinrich Hertz Validates Maxwell Experimentally

1887–1888 · Karlsruhe, Germany

Hertz produces and detects electromagnetic waves in the laboratory, providing direct experimental confirmation of Maxwell's predictions and locking theory to measurable field reality.

🛠️

Practical Three-Phase Motor Architecture Matures

1889 · Polyphase Motive Power Becomes Practical

Three-phase rotating-field motor architecture reaches practical industrial form, helping move AC from demonstration to scalable motive-power infrastructure.

⚙️

Westinghouse Secures Tesla Polyphase Motor Patents

1888 · Polyphase AC Patent Platform

Tesla's AC motor and polyphase system patents are purchased and licensed by Westinghouse, accelerating practical industrial AC drives and transforming patent breakthroughs into deployable machinery.

🏔️

Ames Powers Gold King with Long-Distance AC

July 19, 1891 · Telluride Region, Colorado · 3,000V over about 2.5 miles

Ames transmits 3,000-volt AC about 2.5 miles to the Gold King operation, where a 100-hp motor on the Tesla-Westinghouse AC platform drives ore-processing work. This marks the timeline anchor for the industrial reactive-energy era, where field turbulence becomes a persistent operational concern.

🏢

General Electric Is Formed

1892 · Edison General Electric + Thomson-Houston Merger

General Electric forms through merger, consolidating major electrical manufacturing, generation, and transmission capabilities into one industrial force shaping utility-scale deployment. Elihu Thomson's Thomson-Houston lineage also carried forward practical metering and watt-hour instrumentation work.

🌊

Niagara Falls AC Station Commissioned

1895 · Niagara Falls, New York

The Niagara project enters service with utility-class AC generation and transmission architecture, setting the stage for 1896 long-distance delivery to Buffalo and proving large-scale AC viability.

🧯

Capacitor Correction Enters Industrial Motor Practice

1905 · Lansing, Michigan · Oldsmobile Plant Context

Industrial operators begin applying capacitive correction in motor-heavy environments to calm unstable electrical behavior and improve apparent motor smoothness under reactive conditions.

🌀

Rogowski-Coil Current Sensing Enters the Field Era

1910s (Definitive Publication in 1912) · Walter Rogowski / W. Steinhaus

Flexible coil-based AC current sensing emerges in the 1910s and is definitively described in 1912. The Rogowski-coil principle becomes a long-lived instrumentation path still used in modern field measurements.

🏙️

Reactive Management Becomes Standard Utility Practice

Mid-20th Century · Utility and Plant Correction Programs

Utilities and industrial operators normalize reactive compensation programs using capacitor banks, synchronous solutions, and power-factor programs to manage system symptoms at larger scale.

📟

Digital Metering Begins Exposing Dynamic Behavior

Late-20th Century · Power Quality Instrumentation Expands

Digital monitoring and power-quality instrumentation improve visibility into waveform behavior, but field intelligence remains fragmented and mostly diagnostic rather than continuously orchestrated.

🧪

MPTS Field Harmonization Path Emerges

2001 · Reactive-Field Harmonization Discovery

A new path appears: rather than accepting reactive turbulence as unavoidable, field harmonization is pursued as an intentional engineering objective.

🏭

Ballast-Scale Field Trials and Sales

2001–2006 · Fluorescent Ballast Deployments

Early commercial pathway: harmonization concepts are field-tested and sold in fluorescent lighting ballast applications through 2006.

🏗️

Industrial Facility Redesign

2008 · Platform Reworked for Facility Loads

The architecture is redesigned for heavier industrial environments, shifting from lighting-oriented use cases to broader facility-level electrical behavior control.

📄

Patent Filings Submitted

2009 · IP Protection Phase Begins

Formal patent filings are submitted to protect harmonization methods and their industrial field-control implementations.

Patents Awarded Across 28 Countries

Patent awards expand internationally, securing broad jurisdictional protection for the platform across 28 countries.

2013 · Safety and Compliance Qualification

Regulatory approval milestones are reached with UL and CSA pathways, enabling broader deployment in commercial and industrial environments.

First Production Units and Field Tests

2014 · 65A Production + Initial 100A Units

First 65-amp production units are manufactured and field tests begin, alongside initial 100-amp unit production for expanded operational validation.

🚚

H240 and H490 Delivered to Advanced Sites

2017 · Customer Deployments + NORAD Delivery

H240 and H490 units are delivered for continued field testing at customer sites, with H490 systems delivered to U.S. government operations at NORAD.

How Unity Fits the Timeline

Faraday revealed induction in 1831, and Maxwell gave the field its mathematical language.

By the 1890s, alternating current began powering industrial motors at scale, bringing reactive burden into mines, factories, and manufacturing systems.

For more than a century, operators could observe the symptoms of the reactive field — heat, loss, overload, distortion, and poor power factor — but not the field itself as a living, measurable system.

Capacitors and correction devices helped calm symptoms, but they did not reveal full field behavior in real time.

Beginning in 2001, Unity's MPTS lineage entered that historical gap.

What had long been hidden behind side effects could now be measured, interpreted, and managed as operational value.

Unity's role is not the invention of electricity, and not the invention of correction, but the transition from reactive side effects to reactive intelligence.
