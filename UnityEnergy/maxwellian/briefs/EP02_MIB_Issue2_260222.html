Historical Insight — William Stanley Jr., Great Barrington, 1886

March 20, 1886 — Great Barrington, Massachusetts. William Stanley Jr. energized the first practical AC distribution system in the United States, stepping voltage up for transmission and back down for delivery along Main Street. In a single evening, 23 businesses lit up from a generator a mile away — stores, hotels, a pharmacy — all running on power they could not have touched under the DC paradigm.

Stanley's transformer was the critical wedge: it decoupled generation from load. That decoupling is what made scalable, distributed electrification possible. The architecture he proved that night — generate, transform, transmit, deliver — still underlies every grid operating today.

Unity relevance: Today's grid crisis is the inverse of 1886. We have generation but constrained delivery — transformer shortages, clogged queues, reactive load that eats capacity before it reaches the end customer. Unity's system manages what arrives at the load, recovering headroom without new iron, the same way Stanley recovered reach without new generators.

Current News & Insights: PJM's $11.8B RTEP approval and Goldman's electricity inflation data confirm that the infrastructure gap is now priced into major capital decisions. Unity operates in that gap.
