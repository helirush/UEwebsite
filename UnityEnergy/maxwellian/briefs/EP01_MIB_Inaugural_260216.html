Historical Insight — Gold King Mine, 1891

June 19, 1891 — Telluride, Colorado. At L.L. Nunn's Ames hydroelectric project, Westinghouse/Tesla AC drove a 100-HP induction motor at the Gold King Mine — one of the first industrial demonstrations of alternating current powering heavy machinery. The power traveled nearly three miles over rough terrain, proving that AC could deliver energy where DC simply could not reach.

The motor's magnetizing current established a rotating field: reactive energy showed up on day one. Miners learned fast that phase balance and VARs mattered as much as watts — too little control and the motor stalled; well-tuned, it delivered stable torque from hydro to haulage. That single installation changed the trajectory of electrification, showing that remote generation and long-distance transmission were not only possible but practical.
