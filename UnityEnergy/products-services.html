Unity Service Model

Products and Services: Two Operating Paths for the Same Field System

Every Unity deployment starts with the same installed stack: Measure, Manage (Harmonize), and Exchange. What changes is your operating model after go-live: either your team learns to run the system internally, or Unity continues to monitor and manage field health with you as an ongoing service partner.

Included with every new installed system: Year One service. For the first year after commissioning, Unity provides monitoring support and customer education so your team can either build internal capability or transition into a managed long-term service contract.

Service Track A — Customer-Led Learning Path

Your team becomes field-capable and progressively takes over day-to-day interpretation and operating decisions. Unity provides structured training, operating standards, and periodic technical reviews.

What You Get

Training + Operating Enablement

  • Onboarding workshops for your engineering and operations team.
  • How to read field behavior, burden patterns, and harmonization signals.
  • Decision playbooks for normal operation, anomalies, and escalation.
  • Scheduled Unity review sessions to validate consistency and performance.
Best Fit

Organizations Building Internal Energy Intelligence

  • Teams that want direct ownership of field operations.
  • Sites with internal technical staff and time for training.
  • Programs focused on long-term in-house capability development.

Service Track B — Unity-Managed 24/7 Field Health Path

Unity remains your operating partner after installation. We monitor and interpret field behavior continuously, provide reporting cadence, and coordinate ongoing optimization under service agreement.

What You Get

Managed Monitoring + Response

  • Continuous field monitoring and health oversight.
  • Regular performance reports and burden-risk summaries.
  • Alert escalation workflow and guided corrective actions.
  • Service continuity after Year One through contract options.
Best Fit

Teams That Prefer Managed Outcomes

  • Sites that want minimal internal operating load.
  • Organizations prioritizing continuity and outsourced expertise.
  • Operations that need round-the-clock field health confidence.

How Customers Choose After Year One

During Year One, Unity and your team determine the right long-term model based on staffing, operational complexity, and risk tolerance. At renewal, customers choose one of two contract paths.

Path 1 — Self-Operate with Support

Your team leads daily operation, with Unity retained for scheduled expert support and periodic health reviews.

Path 2 — Full Managed Service Contract

Unity continues full monitoring and field-health management with defined service levels and reporting cadence.

  • Both paths keep the same installed Measure • Manage • Exchange platform.
  • The decision is operating model, not hardware replacement.
  • Contract scope is tailored per site portfolio and operational goals.
