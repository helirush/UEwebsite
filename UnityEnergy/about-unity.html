How Unity Sees the Field

The Silent Symphony

Harmony is not poetry — it is lower conflict inside a working electrical field.

Industrial electricity is more than voltage and current values. It is a coupled field of phase, burden, and harmonic interaction. The 60-hertz waveform sets the base tempo, while every motor, drive, transformer, and resistive load adds a signature to that field.

Over time those signatures can align or conflict. For most of the industrial era, operators could see symptoms but not the full field behavior. The operational result was familiar: reactive burden, harmonic distortion, excess heat, and wasted capacity at conversion.

Unity’s role is to make this behavior measurable and governable in situ. When the field is harmonized, internal conflict drops: less waste, less heat, and more stable operation.
